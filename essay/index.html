What is an Expository Essay?

An expository essay is a type of essay that explains, informs, or describes a topic in a clear and factual way. Its purpose is to help readers understand something, not to persuade them or tell a story. The "Science" section in The New York Times has many examples. Here's one example "Consumers Hate ‘Price Discrimination,’ but They Sure Love a Discount" to be analyzed in the current demo.

The Four Key Aspects

A Clear Thesis

A clear thesis is the main idea of your essay, usually stated in the opening paragraph. It tells the reader exactly what the essay will explain or discuss.

Cohesion

Cohesion refers to how well sentences connect to each other inside a paragraph. Writer use conjunction to organize information.

Coherence

Coherence is about the logical organization of ideas across the whole essay. All paragraph should be supporting the same theme.

Takeaway

Takeaway is the conclusion of the essay. It summarizie the key knowledge that the writer want to share.
