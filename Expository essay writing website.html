What is an Expository Essay?

An expository essay explains a topic clearly and logically. It presents facts, definitions, examples, or steps to help readers understand an idea, process, or concept. The writer stays objective, uses evidence, and organizes information with a clear thesis, supporting paragraphs, and a concise conclusion. No personal opinions dominate. The "Science" section in The New York Times has many examples - Reports on scientific findings are supposed to focus on single topics and explain things from an objective perspective. The Four Key Aspects

A Clear Thesis

Good expository essays generally comes with a clear thesis statement in the opening paragraph - A very straightforward summary of the topic that is consistent with the title.

Cohesion

Good expository essays also build strong connections between every two sentences, which is referred to as cohesive writing. Cohesive writing is the key to organize complex information into simpler and understandable details.

Coherence

For each subtopic in an expository essay, there will be supporting paragraphs. The strategy for connecting these supporting paragraphs in a logical way is known as coherent writing. Coherent writing helps avoid cofusion casued by sudden topic transitions.

Takeaway

Good expository essays always comes with a concise conclusion that helps summarize and reflect on the entire essay. This takeaway is frequently connected to the title to form a logical writing cycle.
